BIOMOD 2019






Therapeutic potential of drugs is limited by the quantity that needs to be delivered. Targeting drug delivery improves treatment efficacy (Peer et al., 2007). Besides, nanomedicines usually cause less dose-limiting side effects than free drugs and have a longer half-life in the bloodstream (Meel et al., 2016). This project aims to exploit advances in DNA nanotechnology to rationally design DNA nanostructures that mimic biological protein nanopores. DNA-based transmembrane nanopores have recently mimicked integral channel proteins in function (Hager et al., 2018), and are easier to design de novo due to the predictability of nucleobase pairing. DNA nanopores can be embedded in the membranes of the drug-encapsulating vesicles and be used as smart drug-delivery systems for personalised medicine. We show that DNA nanopores can be designed to open in response to the presence of a specific disease marker to release the liposome-coated drug.
